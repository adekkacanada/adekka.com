The problem

An AI agent wires funds to the wrong account. A pipeline merges data it was never cleared to merge. Afterward, nobody can prove what was authorized.

Today's controls answer "may this occur?", log the execution somewhere else, and keep no binding between the two. The permission and the outcome live in separate systems, produced by the software being checked. When something goes wrong, reconciling them conclusively is often impossible.

What was authorized

A permission granted at a point in time, against a particular version of the rules, usually recorded by one system.

THE GAP
where failures live
What actually occurred

The operation that ran: its real inputs, transformations, outputs, and delivery, usually logged by another.

NUBAS is being developed to close this gap structurally: every governed authorization is bound to recorded evidence of the operation that followed it.

How it works

Three steps. Every governed action.

01

The action is declared

Before your automated system does anything that matters, the action is written down in machine-readable form: who wants to do what, with which inputs, producing what. No declaration, no execution.

02

Your rules decide

NUBAS checks the declared action against your signed, versioned policies and issues one of six decisions: allow, allow with constraints, hold, escalate to a person, deny, or emergency restrict. The decision comes with a plain-language reason.

03

Proof is created

Two linked records go into a tamper-evident ledger: what was approved, and what actually ran. The evidence is designed so an auditor, regulator, or investigator can verify it without access to your live systems — and without trusting you.

Why you can trust the decision

Deterministic by design. No AI in the decision path.

The workload being governed — including any AI model inside it — sits outside the trust boundary. The layer that judges it does not guess.

  • Same input, same answer. Decisions are rule evaluation, not model inference.
  • Explanations can't hallucinate. Every decision cites the exact rule that fired; no language model exists anywhere in the decision or explanation path.
  • The workload can't cheat. Enforcement happens before your systems receive data, and a compromised workload cannot forge its own receipts.

Verdict Engine Enforcement

Deterministic policy decisions designed for machine-speed execution

  • Six decision types mapped to any domain's dispositions
  • Deterministic: same input, same answer, fired rule cited; no probabilistic model in the path
  • Deployment architecture designed to support local and resource-constrained environments

Evidence Ledger Proof

Paired proofs of authorization and outcome, per operation

  • PROA ⇄ PROOD cryptographically linked in an append-only, hash-chained ledger
  • Lineage query design: intended to trace governed outputs through their recorded operation chain
  • Self-verifying export design: evidence bundles intended to support independent validation without access to the live system

Policy Control Governance

Rules as signed, versioned code, with controlled activation and governed change

  • Versioned policy activation designed to avoid enforcement discontinuity
  • Governance of the governor: rule changes are themselves decided and receipted
  • Governed override: attributed to an authorized actor and recorded as part of the evidence chain

Recall & Remediation Roadmap

When something goes wrong, prove the cleanup

  • Blast radius as a query: forward lineage is intended to identify affected downstream operations represented in the governed record
  • Compensating actions: every remediation step authorized and receipted
  • Honest reversibility: each operation classed as undone, contained, or irreversible, and provably so

Deployment architecture

Deploy where the action happens.

NUBAS places the decision and evidence path inside the environment where your operations run — including environments with no cloud and no connectivity. Enforcement strength is identical everywhere, because the same signed policies execute in every mode.

Managed

Managed for enterprise

Managed NUBAS infrastructure for enterprise applications and agent workloads, with defined tenancy, policy, and evidence controls.

Customer-controlled cloud

Your VPC, your boundaries

Deploy within your VPC or private cloud, with customer-defined network, data-residency, key-custody, and evidence-custody boundaries.

Sovereign on-premises

Inside your perimeter

Organization-owned infrastructure for regulated, restricted, and sovereign environments. Policy authority, keys, and evidence custody remain locally controlled.

Edge & disconnected

No cloud in the loop

Decisions, enforcement, and evidence capture run locally, close to the governed operation. Records synchronize through an authorized connection when connectivity returns.

Local development

Build and evaluate on one machine

Run the full decision and evidence path as a single-node local runtime for development, integration, and policy testing before deploying anywhere else. The local runtime follows the same ActionSpecs semantics and is the target environment for protocol conformance testing.

Deployment modes describe the NUBAS product architecture in development; validated operating envelopes will be published as implementations are tested.

The open protocol

We published the rules before building the product.

A consequential operation must not occur without a policy verdict, and no verdict or outcome may exist without a receipt.

NUBAS implements ActionSpecs, an openly published protocol anyone can read, audit, and implement under CC BY 4.0. The specification is versioned, its changes are logged publicly, and its roadmap is stated. You do not have to take our word for how the system is supposed to behave: it is written down.
